Mango Tree

Scientific Name: Mangifera indica

Classification

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Family: Anacardiaceae
  • Genus: Mangifera
  • Species: Mangifera Indica

Botanical Characteristics

  • Size: Can grow up to 35–40 meters (115–130 feet)
  • Lifespan: Over 300 years
  • Leaves: Evergreen, lance-shaped, leathery; reddish young leaves
  • Flowers: Small, white-pinkish, in dense clusters (panicles)
  • Fruit: Large drupe; green, yellow, red, or mixed
  • Climate: Thrives in tropical and subtropical regions
  • Root System: Deep taproot, widespread lateral roots

Uses

  • Culinary: Ripe mangoes are eaten fresh, juiced, in chutneys/pickles; green ones in salads & drinks
  • Medicinal: Leaves, bark, seeds used for diabetes, diarrhea; rich in vitamins A, C, E & antioxidants
  • Wood: Soft, used in furniture & low-cost construction
  • Cultural: Sacred in India; used in religious rituals and festivals
